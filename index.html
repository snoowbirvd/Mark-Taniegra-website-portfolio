Key Facilities Projects and Contributions

  • Major Build-out and Renovation Projects (2021 - 2022)
    Managed all aspects, including contract drafting, proposal and bill of materials review, and budget alignment with business needs and quality standards. Ensured factors for maintainability, reliability, safety, and compliance were discussed and aligned with the team, secured necessary approvals, and oversaw purchasing processes. Supervised the installation and commissioning of HVAC, electrical systems, and architectural works, and finalized punch-list inspections and corrective actions.
  • Critical UPS Replacement/Upgrade Project (2020)
    Repurposed the CAPEX budget initially allocated for UPS battery replacement (monolithic type) to fund a comprehensive UPS upgrade (modular type), based on lifecycle, energy efficiency, and system reliability analysis. Secured Operations, Client, FM, and Finance approval; assessed work method procedures and risk assessments from vendors, IT change management, and other stakeholder requirements for successful zero-downtime completion, bringing a 40% reduction in preventive maintenance cost annually.
  • Housekeeping Time & Motion Study Improvement Project (2022 - 2023)
    Helped conduct a time and motion study to streamline housekeeping operations, focused on consistent cleaning and sanitation service of the operations floor, office, and common areas. This resulted in reduced idle time with the allocated manpower per area/floor, predictable consumption of cleaning supplies (around 15% reduced idle stock), and consistent positive variance (budget vs. actuals). Also aligned this in the housekeeping playbook for standardized procedures, room arrangements, and new employee guidance.
  • Lighting Improvement Project (2022)
    Led and managed the lighting improvement project on all operations floors, focused on glare control and balanced 300-500 lux levels (55% average increase from existing), aligned with Ops preferences/insights and workplace recommended illuminance levels, all with 12% improved energy consumption.
  • Self-paced Mandatory Eight-hour Safety and Health E-learning Module (2019)
    Customized OSH learning aligned with DOLE-prescribed core modules and the business environment as a safety officer. Ensured the quizzes were not only tailored to our actual specific workplace hazards, but the questions were also designed with cognitive science principles for effective learning for all employees.
